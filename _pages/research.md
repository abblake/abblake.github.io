### New Venture Strategy

My venture research shows how founder personality shapes investor responses, from initial evaluation through negotiation to eventual founder-venture decoupling.

Narcissistic admiration and narcissistic rivalry—two distinct motivational systems—govern how founders pursue or protect status, driving divergent investor responses (*Organization Science*, 2025). These investor responses extend to public markets: CEO charisma increases IPO prices while humility decreases them, demonstrating that personality signals reach even sophisticated market actors (*Strategic Entrepreneurship Journal*, 2023).

In real-time negotiation, these signaling dynamics cut both ways. Across 895 entrepreneurial pitches, I find that linguistic alignment between founders and investors secures deals but costs founders equity—a commitment-concession paradox (under review, *Administrative Science Quarterly*). This coordination, however, is unstable. Ventures eventually outgrow founders as perceptual coupling breaks down at scale, a process I call attunement drift (under review, *Strategic Management Journal*).

Together, these studies reveal that relational dynamics—not founder characteristics alone—determine venture outcomes at every stage.

### Executive Psychology & Methods

A parallel stream examines how executive personality shapes organizational outcomes and advances the methods this work requires.

In a 22-year meta-analysis, I show that agreeableness predicts both leadership emergence and effectiveness (*Leadership Quarterly*, 2022, Best Paper Nominee; featured in *The Economist*). Moving from what personality predicts to how it operates, I examine how CEO psychopathy increases turnover through behavioral residue accumulation (under review, *Administrative Science Quarterly*).

On the methodological side, I develop large-scale software infrastructure for reproducible archival data collection (target: *Academy of Management Journal*), advance open science standards for code and data sharing (*Journal of Management*, 2023), and demonstrate that a widely used ML tool for CEO personality prediction fails out-of-sample against independent benchmarks (target: *Strategic Management Journal*).

### Corporate Governance

A study under review at *Academy of Management Journal* examines how board chair narcissistic rivalry drives control-oriented governance—monitoring, boundary enforcement—over collaboration-oriented governance. A theoretically consequential asymmetry emerges: admiration does not promote collaboration—it only constrains rivalry's control-seeking.
